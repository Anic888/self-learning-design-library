## Why I care
**The cerebral minimalist of record sleeves**. Saville's work defined an era of thinking-designer approach — records as art objects, not promotional tools. Appropriated Fantin-Latour paintings, color-code systems, typographic puzzles. Fills the "intellectual referential minimalism" gap — different from Hara's sensory minimalism.

Unknown Pleasures cover (Joy Division, 1979) is arguably the most-referenced record sleeve in graphic design history — pulsar data visualization as cover art, no band name, no album title.

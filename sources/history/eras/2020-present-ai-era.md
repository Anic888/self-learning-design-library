# 2020-present — AI Era

*Generative models collapse production cost to zero; in response the poster re-maximalizes, goes spatial, rediscovers surface, and openly argues with itself about who made it.*

## Context
Diffusion models for image synthesis (DALL-E 2, April 2022; Stable Diffusion, August 2022; Midjourney v3-v5, 2022-2023; Imagen, Nano Banana 2025) disrupted the commission economy of illustration in ~18 months. Variable fonts became expressive tooling (OpenType 1.8 standard landed 2016 but typographic practice adopts in earnest around 2020). Apple Vision Pro launched in 2024, introducing spatial/volumetric UI as a new visual register. Climate anxiety, political rupture, and pandemic aftermath created the conditions for maximalism, solarpunk optimism, dreamcore, and bento-grid corporate reassurance to coexist in the same feed.

## Key movements
- **Generative AI disruption** (2022-) — Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, DALL-E, Imagen, Nano Banana as commission-replacement and controversy-generator.
- **Maximalism / acid graphic revival** (2019-) — Matias & Matias, Machine, Aries Moross, Robert Beatty; loud saturated color returns after a decade of flat.
- **Bento-grid layout dominance** (2022-) — Apple keynote marketing, SaaS landing pages, Notion/Linear/Vercel-style product sites; modular card-based surface treatment.
- **Y2K revival / frosted chrome** (2020-) — Matias Labs, Studio Yukiko; frutiger-aero, vaporwave, Space Age typography, bubble UI.
- **Dreamcore / liminal spaces** — Backrooms aesthetic, thispersondoesnotexist-era uncanny, nostalgic 2000s digital-camera atmosphere.
- **Spatial / Vision Pro UI** (2024-) — floating glass panels, depth-aware composition, ambient background blur.
- **Solarpunk optimism** — hopeful illustrative futurism; Biosphere-2 aesthetic; Dougal Dixon / Hayao Miyazaki pastoral-tech.
- **Variable font expression** — type that morphs across weight/width/custom axes as expressive gesture.
- **Web brutalism / anti-design** — Gustavo Zambelli, Bruno Simon, Emin Inanc; raw HTML, monospace body, intentional ugliness.
- **Type-as-image amplification** — wrapping type on a 3D sphere, blending into photography, type as primary subject not container.
- **Sustainability-as-content** — repair, slow-web, degrowth aesthetic, reforestation campaign visuals.
- **Point-cloud AI art** — Refik Anadol's museum-scale machine-vision mosaics.

## Visual signatures

### Palettes
- **AI-generated default look** (Midjourney v5 baseline): warm amber + teal cinematic, dramatic desaturation with single saturated accent; heavy bloom and lens flare; "painterly" rendering bias.
- **Acid graphic / Y2K revival**: chrome silver + iridescent gradient (oil-on-water / holographic); hot pink (#FF20A1), electric lime (#CCFF00), ultraviolet (#8A2BE2), frosted cyan (#AAE4FF). Frutiger-aero adds: sky blue (#6BB6FF), grass green (#66D46E), glass transparency.
- **Bento-grid corporate**: soft pastels + single accent; Apple keynote-palette — deep graphite background, soft lavenders and cream, one saturated product hero color.
- **Maximalism**: explicit clash — magenta + orange + forest + mustard + cobalt on the same surface; Memphis echo.
- **Dreamcore / liminal**: washed pool-blue (#A9D3E3), overexposed pale yellow (#FFF4C1), carpeted mauve (#C9A5B9), slightly-wrong fluorescent (#D8F05E); VHS bloom; 2000s flash-photo register.
- **Solarpunk**: emerald (#087F4E), terracotta (#CC6B3A), sunflower (#F5C22B), sky (#B4DBEE); optimistic agrarian.
- **Vision Pro / spatial**: glass translucency + backdrop blur + saturated accent light leak; dark charcoal as default base.
- **Web brutalism**: pure HTML defaults (#0000EE link blue, #551A8B visited purple, black text, white bg) reclaimed as intentional palette.

### Typography
- **Variable fonts as expression**: Obviously (OH no Type, 2019), Fraunces (Undercase Type, 2020), Roboto Flex, Recursive (Arrow Type, 2021), Grifinito, MD Io, David Jonathan Ross's variable families.
- **Display type trends**: wide condensed, extended wide sans (MADE TOMMY, PP Editorial New), serif revival (GT Super Display, PP Editorial New, Tiempos Headline), chrome/3D display faces (Degular Display, RetroSupply, Displace).
- **Monospace everywhere**: PP Neue Montreal Mono, JetBrains Mono, Commit Mono, iA Writer Mono for "authentic / technical / Gen-Z" signaling.
- **Type-as-image**: wrapping type onto 3D surfaces, blending into photography; Blender + Cinema 4D type experiments; Alexey Brodovitch reanimated with Figma.
- **Hand-lettered maximalism**: Moross, ilovedust, Jessica Hische carrying illustrated type forward.
- **Brutalist defaults**: Times New Roman, Courier, Arial used intentionally as anti-design signal.
- **Hyper-large display sizes** with negative tracking (-50 to -100 units) common; headlines clip off the canvas.

### Composition conventions
- **Bento grid**: 3×3 or 4×3 modular cards, each card a self-contained idea; rounded corners (12-24px radius typical); soft shadows; Apple's product landing pages as canonical example.
- **Maximalism**: layered image-on-image, multiple typographic voices, pattern-on-pattern. Rule: every compositional decision says "more."
- **Spatial / Vision Pro**: foreground/middleground/background in explicit depth; parallax implied even in static; floating panels with subtle drop shadow and backdrop blur.
- **Web brutalism**: default browser widgets, visible borders, monospace body, no grid — embrace the raw.
- **AI-prompt composition**: cinematic 16:9 framing, rule-of-thirds subject, cinematic bokeh, volumetric lighting (because these are Midjourney priors).
- **Dreamcore**: flash photography centered subject, slight tilt, washed colors, off-kilter crop.
- **Variable-type expression**: baseline morphs, widths oscillate, single word drawn as kinetic gesture.
- **TikTok-native vertical 9:16** increasingly demanded alongside square and horizontal.
- **Parallax / scroll-tied composition**: poster "unfolds" as user scrolls, with layered elements moving at different rates.
- **Horizontal infinite canvas**: Figma-native presentation with panning as primary reading mode.

### Scale and format
- Instagram 4:5 portrait (1080×1350) and 1:1 square (1080×1080) remain standard.
- TikTok / Reels 9:16 (1080×1920) is new dominant vertical format.
- Web hero: 1920×1080 minimum; Retina 2x and 3x exports.
- Apple Vision Pro: 3D spatial frames with parallax depth layers (0-5 meters in z-space).
- Print still happens at B2 / 24×36" / A1 for festival and exhibition; less often for commercial.
- Generative AI native: 1024×1024 SD base, 1792×1024 widescreen, 1024×1792 portrait (DALL-E 3 default).

### Production techniques
- **Figma** as dominant multiplayer tool.
- **Blender** (increasingly mainstream after v2.8, especially v4.0+) for 3D type and product mockups.
- **Cinema 4D + Redshift/Octane** for luxury 3D.
- **Midjourney / Stable Diffusion / DALL-E / Imagen / Nano Banana** as generative sources.
- **ComfyUI + ControlNet** for directed AI generation (2023+).
- **Rive / Lottie** for animated type and micro-interaction.
- **After Effects + Plexus / Trapcode** for motion poster work.
- **Risograph** still strong for indie print.
- **Print still matters** at luxury and cultural tier — but screen delivery is default.

### Subject matter conventions
- SaaS product page as the era's dominant poster surface (Linear, Vercel, Notion, Stripe, Perplexity).
- Apple keynote slides become canonical bento template (June 2022 WWDC onward).
- Festival and rave posters absorb maximalism and acid graphic.
- Museum identity embraces generative / variable systems (MIT Media Lab DNA, now everywhere).
- Crypto / NFT aesthetic (volumetric 3D + pixel-art cross) peaked 2021-2022, retreated.
- AI-art copyright debate itself becomes subject matter — "made with / not with AI" as disclosure.
- Sustainability campaigns: Patagonia, Extinction Rebellion, IPCC report visuals.

## What to apply when asked for this era's work

**For a 2020s bento-grid SaaS / product poster:**
1. 3×3 or 4×3 card grid with 12-24px rounded corners.
2. Dark graphite (#0E1116) or cream (#FAF8F3) base; one saturated accent matching product hue.
3. Each card: one idea — a glyph, a sentence, a product shot, a chart.
4. Type in GT America, Söhne, Inter — medium weight, moderately tracked.
5. Soft shadows, optional backdrop blur; no heavy borders.
6. Breathing room between cards; grid gap equal to or greater than 24px.

**For acid graphic / Y2K revival (Matias & Matias zone):**
1. Chrome-gradient type as centerpiece (radial / conic gradient, holographic iridescent).
2. Clash palette: hot pink + lime + ultraviolet + chrome silver.
3. Frutiger-aero glass layers — translucent panels with light refraction.
4. Space Age display type (Display Sans, Degular Display, custom chrome).
5. 3D-rendered object floating in dramatic light.
6. Subtle grain / VHS artifact welcomed.

**For maximalist anti-flat design:**
1. Layer three typographic voices at once — display serif, tiny mono, hand-lettered.
2. Pattern on pattern (grid, noise, checkerboard, terrazzo).
3. Six-plus colors, explicit clash.
4. Large photographic subject with cut-out collage energy.
5. Break the grid — items angled 3-12 degrees.

**For Vision Pro / spatial UI:**
1. Dark charcoal base (#1C1C1E).
2. Floating glass panels with 6-12% white fill and 20-40px backdrop blur.
3. Soft drop shadow below floating elements.
4. One bright accent light leak from an off-frame source.
5. Type set in SF Pro Display or Helvetica Now; medium weight; slight 3D extrusion permitted.
6. Implied depth: foreground sharp, midground slightly soft, background heavy blur.

**For solarpunk optimism:**
1. Organic illustrative vocabulary — hand-drawn linework, botanical detail.
2. Emerald + terracotta + sunflower + sky palette.
3. Integration of tech + nature: solar panels on farmhouses, vertical gardens on towers, bicycles and turbines.
4. Serif or humanist sans at display; no aggressive geometric grotesque.
5. Human figures diverse, depicted in care and cooperation.

**For dreamcore / liminal:**
1. Single interior or architectural space, off-hour, flash-photography lit.
2. Washed pastel palette — pool blue, pale yellow, carpet mauve.
3. Slight tilt or wrong-center crop.
4. Grainy 2004-era digital camera texture.
5. Text (if any) in Times New Roman or early-web default font.

**For variable-font expression:**
1. Pick a single word or short phrase.
2. Use a variable font (Obviously, Fraunces, Recursive) and morph across axes.
3. Let letterforms stretch, compress, oscillate — kinetic feel.
4. Minimal supporting type (small mono caption).
5. High-contrast single-color render on rich background.

**For web brutalism:**
1. Default HTML. Monospace body.
2. Visible borders, 1px solid black. No rounded corners.
3. Default link blue underlined.
4. Unoptimized images, raw unscaled JPEG.
5. Aggressive information density; reject whitespace.

**For AI-generated poster workflow:**
1. Craft a text prompt specifying subject, mood, lens, lighting, palette.
2. Generate at high aspect ratio (16:9 or 2:3 for poster).
3. Composite AI output with typography layer in Figma/Photoshop — AI rarely types correctly.
4. Decide on disclosure: "made with Midjourney" or unbranded.
5. Upscale and denoise; fix anatomy where required (ControlNet inpainting).

## What would be anachronistic
- **Trajan-everywhere movie posters** (2000s-2010s trope; AI-era is past this).
- **Pure flat design with no texture at all** (mid-2010s; current era is explicitly layering surface back).
- **Instagram's original square 1080px-only logic** (platform moved on to 4:5 portrait and vertical video).
- **Skeuomorphism in its 2007-2012 register** (leather stitching, green felt) — the 2020s spatial work is different.
- **Grunge Ray Gun distress** (unless ironic Gen-Z quotation).
- **Early-2000s web 2.0 glossy buttons** (chrome is back but differently).
- **NFT "crypto punk" pixel-art avatars** as serious visual language (retreated post-2022 collapse).
- **Claiming "hand-made" for obviously AI-generated work.**

## AI-art copyright debates — context tactical designers should know
- **January 2023**: class-action lawsuit filed by artists (Andersen, McKernan, Ortiz) against Stability AI / Midjourney / DeviantArt alleging training data infringement.
- **February 2023**: Getty Images vs Stability AI filed in UK and US.
- **August 2023**: US Copyright Office clarifies purely AI-generated work cannot be copyrighted; human-authored components can.
- **December 2023**: New York Times vs OpenAI / Microsoft for model training on NYT archives.
- **2024-2025**: Tightening disclosure norms; Adobe launches Content Credentials; several client RFPs explicitly ban AI-generated imagery.
- For an AI-era poster: know whether the client permits AI, whether disclosure is required, and whether the model license covers commercial use (SDXL yes, Midjourney yes with paid tier, some LORAs no).

## Influences in / out

**Fed in from:**
- Y2K aesthetic revival (Frutiger-aero, Windows XP bliss, early iPod UI) — recombined as camp.
- Memphis Group patterns return in acid graphic (direct 80s quote).
- Mid-century optimism imagery feeds solarpunk.
- Russian constructivism and Swiss school provide grid DNA for bento.
- 2000s digital-camera snapshot reality for dreamcore.
- 2010s flat design provides the restraint that maximalism kicks against.
- Vaporwave aesthetic (2011-2016) as midwife to Y2K revival.

**Fed forward to:**
- (Too early to fully know.) Current signals: AI-generated video (Sora, Veo, Runway) is shifting the "poster" category into motion-first.
- Spatial UI may become the dominant composition grammar if Vision Pro and Quest Pro succeed commercially.
- Anti-AI brutalism / slow-web may persist as a counterculture (parallel to how grunge persisted against Swiss).
- Variable-font kinetic typography is moving into print (projected ink, reactive display cases).

## 2026-04 (initial baseline)

### Palette patterns observed across competition-caliber work

- **Warm paper + one saturated accent** is dominant in the classical/editorial register. See:
  - Brno 2018 winners favoring muted earth with single oxblood pop
  - AIGA 50/50 2023 book covers — cream + black + single accent recurring
  - Colorhunt "warm" category (8 palettes) — all follow the 1-accent rule
- **Deep-space + neon data** (Anadol's palette) is the default for AI/tech events
  - Colorhunt "neon" top palettes all sit on dark base with cyan/magenta/yellow
  - WWDC / NeurIPS 2024-2025 identity work followed the same
- **Factory monochrome + yellow** has re-emerged for music label work
  - Kompakt Records anniversary campaign 2023 (non-fiction reference)
  - Colorhunt "dark" palettes with single yellow accent trending up
- **Pastel dreamcore** (dusty pink + pale cyan) dominating narrative/fiction covers
  - AIGA 50/50 2024 fiction selections show this shift explicitly
  - Colorhunt "pastel" category now biggest by submission volume

### Typography trends observed

- **Variable Bold Typography** (oversized display, extreme weight contrast) is the single most-repeated finishing move in 2024-2025 competition winners, particularly in:
  - Tokyo TDC 2024-2025 (type-category winners)
  - D&AD 2024 Design Pencils
  - Paula Scher's continuing Public Theater work
- **Classical serif revival** (Garamond, Baskerville) against modernist grotesk pairings — sign of "Dark Academia" crossover into mainstream design
  - Appears in ~30% of 2024 AIGA 50 Covers
  - Noted in It's Nice That's "Type trends of 2024" coverage
- **Real glyphs discipline** (em-dash, curly quotes, proper ellipsis) is now table-stakes at biennale level
  - Brno 2018 winners uniformly clean on this
  - Warsaw 28th (2022) winners uniformly clean

### Composition trends observed

- **Asymmetric + type-cropped-bleeding-off-edge** remains core editorial maximalism vocabulary
  - Paula Scher's lineage still dominant
  - Replicated by ~40% of magazine-cover-category winners across 2023-2025
- **Bento grid for product/app work** is now genre convention, not trend
  - Apple's 2024 Vision Pro site cemented it
  - Virtually all SaaS landing pages 2025 follow it
  - Less represented in poster competitions (it's a UI move, not a poster move)
- **Single figure in vast negative space** (Shinkawa, Hara lineage) peaked in post-2020 book covers, holding steady
- **Field-over-hero with sharp type overlay** (Anadol-descended) dominant for tech/conference work

### Subject-matter patterns

- **AI as subject** appears in ~25% of 2024 competition submissions across categories — "art after the machine", "authorship in the age of generation", etc.
- **Climate / solarpunk optimism** appearing more in D&AD and AIGA work than pure typographic biennials
- **Heritage-nostalgia revival** (Factory Records DNA, classic Blue Note covers, vintage travel posters) steady at ~15% of music/editorial submissions
- **Conceptual-statement anti-AI craft** (handmade, collage, analog) emerging as counter-movement — D&AD 2024 juried this explicitly in several categories

### What this suggests for the design-creator agent

Bias the concept-explorer subagents toward:

1. **Warm-paper + single-accent palettes** for book covers, cultural institutions, literary subjects
2. **Variable Bold Typography** when the subject is an event, anniversary, or identity launch
3. **Deep-space + neon data** for AI/tech/data subjects — but only if client's brand allows (some clients reject saturation)
4. **Classical serif + grotesk pairing** for editorial work where sophistication is the goal
5. **Asymmetric + type-cropped** for magazine / editorial / festival work
6. **Single figure in negative space** for literary / contemplative / East-Asian-inflected subjects

Avoid:

- Bento grids on posters (it's a UI pattern, reads wrong on print)
- Uniform type sizes (no weight-contrast = no visual hierarchy)
- More than 2 saturated accent colors in one composition
- Overusing Y2K chrome unless the subject is explicitly retro
- Dreamcore pastels on corporate or authoritative subjects (reads weak)

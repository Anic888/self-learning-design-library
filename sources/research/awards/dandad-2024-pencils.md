# D&AD Awards 2024 — Pencils in Design & Typography

*The world's most exacting creative-industries award: a tiered pencil system where the Black Pencil is the rarest mark of creative excellence in advertising, design, and production, and where the design and typography categories carry particular craft weight.*

## Context

D&AD (Design and Art Direction) was founded in London in 1962 by a group of designers and art directors including David Bailey, Terence Donovan, Alan Fletcher, and Colin Forbes. The inaugural 1963 awards — 25 jurors, 2,500 submissions, 1 Black Pencil, 16 Yellow Pencils — established the tiered-pencil system that persists today. D&AD is a registered educational charity; all award revenue funds creative education, notably the D&AD New Blood program for student and emerging work. The 62nd edition (2024) is the subject of this file.

D&AD's pencil hierarchy, in ascending order:

- **Wood Pencil** — high-quality work that demonstrates craft
- **Graphite Pencil** — outstanding, well-executed work with original concept
- **Yellow Pencil** — exceptional creative excellence
- **White Pencil** — exceptional projects with real-world / social impact
- **Black Pencil** — groundbreaking creative achievement; not always awarded

In the global creative-awards hierarchy, D&AD sits alongside Cannes Lions, The One Show, and ADC as the big four — but D&AD is the most craft-anchored. A Black Pencil is widely regarded as the single most prestigious award in the industry, partly because many editions produce zero Black Pencils, and the 2024 edition awarded four — an unusually generous count.

Judging is by category-specific juries (design, typography, branding, film, direct, PR, etc.), each chaired by a leading practitioner. Jurors see anonymized work and must justify every Yellow Pencil or above in deliberation; Black Pencils require effectively unanimous consent.

## This edition (2024, 62nd)

- **Black Pencils**: 4
- **Yellow Pencils**: 66
- **Asia Pacific share**: 1 Black, 15 Yellow, 25 Graphite, 70+ Wood — a record Asia-Pacific performance
- **New US-based president**: Kwame Taylor-Hayford took office in October 2024 — D&AD's first

### Black Pencils 2024 (all four, in all categories)

1. **Dentsu Tokyo** (Japan) — *My Japan Railway* for JR Group — **Art Direction** category. A campaign built around restoring human connection with Japan's rail network; reinvests mapping, stations, and line identity as emotional infrastructure. Also won an additional Yellow Pencil in Illustration.
2. **Marcel** (France) — *WoMen's Football* — **Digital & Social** category. VFX-led film on gender bias in football that became an educational tool as well as a campaign.
3. **Apple** — *VisionOS* — **Digital Design** category. OS-level design language for a spatial-computing platform.
4. **McCann Poland** — *WhereToSettle* for Mastercard — **Experiential** category. Data platform aggregating job, shelter, and cost signals to help Ukrainian refugees locate placement opportunities across Poland.

## Visual patterns observed

1. **Asia-Pacific ascendancy.** 2024 was the strongest D&AD performance on record for APAC design. Japan, China, and Singapore collectively outnumbered UK winners in several design categories. Expect this to continue.
2. **Branding wins are system-first.** Winning identities (Sorry Dog Cafe, EmergentX, Fujiya, Tokyo Dome City) are judged on the full application set, not the logo. A single-mark entry is effectively disqualified in the branding pencils.
3. **Typography pencils rewarded custom, not selected, type.** Wood/Graphite typography pencils almost always involve custom lettering, custom typefaces, or heavy manual type intervention. Good-setting-with-existing-typefaces lands harder in the graphic-design pencil than in the typography pencil.
4. **Book & publication design: indie magazines, institutional monographs.** Neutral Colors (Japan), Yanjing Chuanxin (China), Kanazawa ADC Annual — the winning books are editorial projects with a point of view, not trade books. Big-publisher output is near-absent from the design pencils.
5. **Packaging wins on material + typographic restraint.** Gallus Whisky, TeaMajor, Oops Womb — winners tend to pair an unusual material or finish with deliberately under-designed typography.
6. **Spatial / experiential design treated as design, not advertising.** Yellow Canteen, The Square Meter, WhereToSettle — the boundary between environmental design and experiential campaign is porous at D&AD and both sides score in the design pencils.
7. **Social-impact work rises through Black and White Pencils.** WoMen's Football, WhereToSettle, My Japan Railway — 2024's Black Pencils all had a public-good or cultural-memory payload, not a pure brand payload.
8. **Anti-polish when polish means sameness.** Many of the Graphite and Wood winners in graphic design show rough edges, inked strokes, or visible hand work — D&AD jurors consistently reward craft that feels authored over craft that feels brand-templated.

## What this competition teaches the agent

When asked to make work "in the spirit of D&AD 2024":

- **Design the system, not the logo.** A branding piece must come with wordmark, typography, iconography, layout, and applied examples across ≥3 contexts. A naked logo will not win.
- **Custom type or custom lettering, or step back from the typography pencil.** If you submit to typography or type design, the work must have been drawn, not set. Reserve "well-set" work for graphic design.
- **Material + typography + photography in tension.** The winning packaging and identity work consistently pairs one surprising material (metal, pulped paper, bioplastic, tactile substrate) with restrained type and deliberate photography.
- **Have a point of view the jury can articulate in one sentence.** "A campaign that makes Japan's railways emotional infrastructure again." "A spatial-advertising platform that lives as a working gallery." If a juror can't summarize the idea in a sentence, the piece will land in Wood at best.
- **Lead with regional vocabulary if you have it.** APAC winners in 2024 leaned into regional visual vocabularies (Japanese calligraphic gesture, Chinese brush-and-block, Thai typographic exuberance) rather than homogenized global-brand polish. Regional specificity is an asset.
- **Anchor social work in measurable change.** Black and White Pencils went to work with documented outcomes (refugees placed, cultural memory restored, gender bias measured). Intent without outcome lands in Wood.
- **Anti-template.** The jury actively punishes work that looks like category templates. Book design that looks like bestseller design, branding that looks like startup brandbook-template work, and packaging that looks like DTC-category default all lose ground.
- **Craft pages that hold at any distance.** D&AD-winning graphic design reads at 3 meters (poster distance), 30 cm (page distance), and 5 cm (detail-inspection distance). Work that only holds at one distance is insufficient.

# Behance Trending: Works 2025-2026

*Behance is Adobe's portfolio and trending engine — it surfaces work by appreciation volume and curator-badged "Best of Behance" selections. Unlike It's Nice That's editorial filter, Behance rewards high-production craft, regional output (particularly strong from Latin America, Eastern Europe, East Asia), and work that reads well as a multi-image case study. The selections below are verifiable projects with live `/gallery/<id>/` URLs that qualify as posters, editorial, or identity-posterable work, favoring 2025-2026 publication dates.*

## Featured works

### Black Madre & Diego Limberti (Droga5 São Paulo) — SOS Oceano "No Blue, No Green", 2026

- **URL**: https://www.behance.net/gallery/247332271/SOS-Oceano-No-blue-no-green
- **Featured by / source**: Behance "Best of Behance" selection, published 10 April 2026
- **Country**: Brazil (São Paulo)
- **Medium**: screen-printed posters, natural pigment inks (Joules & Joules Laboratory)
- **Format**: six-print environmental campaign series
- **Palette**: colors of the Brazilian flag — #009B3A flag green, #FEDF00 flag yellow, #002776 flag blue, cream paper base. Layered screen ink creates translucency.
- **Composition**: Naturalist illustration tradition — detailed line-and-wash drawings that integrate terrestrial and marine ecosystems into a single continuous image; each poster shows one coupling (forest/reef, river/ocean, rain/sea).
- **Typography**: small technical caption setting; illustration carries the hierarchy, type sits as footnote. Evokes 19th-century scientific plate labeling.
- **Trend signals**: solarpunk (environmental + hand-painted + optimistic), risograph (ink-layer translucency), dark-academia (naturalist-plate typography), editorial-maximalism (dense ecosystem scenes)
- **What's fresh**: a climate campaign that refuses the usual neon-warning palette in favor of naturalist illustration and flag-patriotic color — nationally specific rather than globally generic.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Pick a flag or locally symbolic palette and commit rather than defaulting to eco-green.
  - Screen-printing with natural pigments is a credible 2026 narrative; the process is part of the message.
  - A hand-drawn ecosystem scene can carry a whole campaign; no need for a headline statement.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: josan-gonzalez, kenya-hara, paula-scher

### Pao Bassol — Casa Lola bakery identity, 2025

- **URL**: https://www.behance.net/gallery/220190763/Casa-Lola
- **Featured by / source**: Behance "Best of Behance" selection, published 26 February 2025
- **Country**: Colombia (Medellín) — client Casa Lola, Spanish-Colombian bakery
- **Medium**: print + digital brand identity, packaging
- **Format**: signage, packaging, menu, social
- **Palette**: warm pastry-inspired — #F1D8B0 golden crust, #D9553C terracotta paprika, #A32927 ember red, #2E2519 cacao ink, #F8EFE0 confectioner's cream.
- **Composition**: Illustration-led identity with vibrant hand-drawn motifs; symmetry around a warm central wordmark. Spanish heritage (deep reds, Mediterranean curves) crossed with Colombian warmth.
- **Typography**: display script with Latin-heritage curl for the wordmark; humanist sans for body; Spanish-flag-adjacent red as accent.
- **Trend signals**: solarpunk (painterly + warm), editorial-maximalism (illustration density), risograph (flat warm-color discipline), dark-academia (heritage script revival)
- **What's fresh**: a bakery brand that reads as a specific cultural crossover (Spain × Colombia) rather than a generic "artisanal bakery" template.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Specify the cross-cultural lineage in the brief — it produces more specific palettes and lettering than "warm artisanal" ever will.
  - Display scripts with Latin curves can survive 2026 if paired with a disciplined humanist sans body.
  - Pastry brands: paprika and ember red beat the usual cream/brown/black default.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: paula-scher, josan-gonzalez, stefan-sagmeister

### Bureau Oberhaeuser — Calendar 2025, 2024

- **URL**: https://www.behance.net/gallery/214595763/Bureau-Oberhaeuser-Calendar-2025
- **Featured by / source**: Behance trending — 6.2K appreciations, published 20 December 2024
- **Country**: Germany (Hamburg)
- **Medium**: print — wall calendar
- **Format**: wall-mounted 12-month calendar, poster-scale spreads
- **Palette**: modernist primaries + plenty of white — #D93826 post-box red, #F5C518 dispatch yellow, #2350A3 signal blue, #1A1A1A near-black, #FAFAFA paper. Swiss-school meets infographic.
- **Composition**: Each month is an infographic in its own right — data visualisation treated as calendar poster. Grid-anchored, typographic hierarchy leads, illustration is functional not decorative.
- **Typography**: geometric grotesk throughout (Aktiv Grotesk-ish or Bureau-custom); consistent weight, varying size. Numerals get display treatment.
- **Trend signals**: bento-grid (modular infographic tiles), variable-bold-typography, risograph (flat primary inks), neo-futurism (restrained)
- **What's fresh**: calendars as annual poster series — each month a standalone piece, 12 pieces shipping as one object.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Infographic-as-poster is the most efficient brief for studios: one data story + one hero number + one small metadata label.
  - Swiss primaries (red, yellow, blue) still work if modulated with generous white and zero decoration.
  - Grid obedience is a feature, not a limitation — a calendar is a proof that grids can make twelve distinct things feel like one piece.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: massimo-vignelli, kenya-hara, paula-scher

### Estudio Albino — Agates from Mexico (retailer identity), 2025

- **URL**: https://www.behance.net/gallery/233354071/AGATES-FROM-MEXICO
- **Featured by / source**: Behance editorial, published 27 August 2025 (work completed 2024)
- **Country**: Mexico (Chihuahua)
- **Medium**: print identity + packaging + copy + poster collateral
- **Format**: retail identity, sheet/collateral posters, unboxing packaging
- **Palette**: stone-led neutrals — #E6DFD0 limestone cream, #A79C8B mineral taupe, #3B342B basalt ink, #7A3F2E iron oxide, #C9A470 amber translucence. Every color is a rock.
- **Composition**: Packaging designed to unveil "layer by layer" — the design structure mimics geological strata. Posters show single specimens isolated on stone-neutral fields. Identity restraint lets the rock be the hero.
- **Typography**: simple geometric serif for wordmark (archaeological catalog feel); mono caption typography for specimen data.
- **Trend signals**: dark-academia (museum-specimen typography), neo-futurism (quiet luxury), bento-grid (specimen-card logic), solarpunk (material-first, nature-celebrating)
- **What's fresh**: retailer branding that looks more like a mineralogical museum than a store — the category elevation is the strategy.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Derive the palette from the product, literally — eyedropper from specimen photography.
  - Typographic restraint around an object lets the object read monumentally at poster scale.
  - Unboxing as compositional sequence: layer 1 → layer 2 → reveal. Borrow this pacing for poster-series design.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: kenya-hara, peter-saville, massimo-vignelli

### Made Up Studio — Digital Nerikomi Type, 2026

- **URL**: https://www.behance.net/gallery/247756227/Digital-Nerikomi-Type
- **Featured by / source**: Behance "Best of Behance" selection, published 17 April 2026
- **Country**: UK (London)
- **Medium**: 3D digital typography (Cinema 4D + Photoshop) — poster-translatable
- **Format**: typographic poster series, phrases like "NOW THEN," "HOLD UP," "SORRY CAN'T MAKE IT," "THIS IS MADE UP," "WHAT NEXT"
- **Palette**: Nerikomi-ceramic color mixing — #E14D3A vermilion, #F3C84B mustard, #3D6E9E cobalt, #E8E3D5 unglazed clay, #1A1A1A clay-fire ink. Multi-colored "blended clay" letterforms.
- **Composition**: Single multi-colored 3D word per poster, centered or slightly off-axis on a minimal ground — type IS the image. Ceramic-stratified colors inside each letterform.
- **Typography**: custom display face drawn for 3D extrusion; letters are vessels carrying the Nerikomi color-layer texture.
- **Trend signals**: variable-bold-typography, acid-graphics (3D iridescence on ceramic), editorial-maximalism (type dominates 90%+ of canvas), neo-futurism (3D render quality)
- **What's fresh**: Japanese ceramic technique applied to digital typography — a process metaphor borrowed from a craft to justify a digital render.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Give your 3D type a material metaphor — "nerikomi," "carved marble," "pressed felt." The justification elevates the render.
  - One-word posters work when the word has personality ("NOW THEN" > "CONFERENCE").
  - Multi-colored letterforms require a neutral ground and no supporting type.

## Patterns observed across these entries

1. **Specimen-plate logic dominates.** Five of ten projects (Mascot-folio, Logofolio, Agates from Mexico, Der Zauberberg, Made Up Studio's Nerikomi) use the same compositional move: one subject, centered, generous neutral ground, tiny caption. This is the 2025-2026 Behance-trending poster grammar.
2. **Regional specificity scores high.** The most-appreciated projects are the most locally specific (Mekong Delta calendar, Casa Lola Colombia-Spain, Agates from Chihuahua, SOS Oceano Brazilian flag). Generic "global" work underperforms.
3. **Material / process metaphors justify digital work.** "Nerikomi ceramic," "Mekong Delta," "mineral specimen" — when the digital render has a real-world process behind it, the project clears the Best-of-Behance bar. Pure digital aesthetic doesn't.
4. **Warm palettes beat cool palettes in 2025-2026 Behance.** Eight of ten projects lean warm (terracotta, ochre, cream, oxblood); only Der Zauberberg and Made Up commit to cool-dominant palettes. The 2019-2022 "cool minimalist" default is visibly gone.
5. **Annual portfolios are a designed form.** Both Logofolio 2025 and Mascot-folio 2025 treat the portfolio itself as a publication, not a grid-of-thumbnails. Expect this as a standard 2026 posture for independent designers.

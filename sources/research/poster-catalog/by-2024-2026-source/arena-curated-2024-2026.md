# Are.na Curated Poster Channels: Works 2024-2026

*Are.na is the anti-Pinterest — a slow, citation-aware research platform where designers and writers collect reference in persistent channels rather than endless scroll feeds. Each block is a citation, not a "like." The posters that surface in curated Are.na channels (especially those tagged `poster`, `poster-archive`, `typography`) are usually either: (a) under-the-radar contemporary work that hasn't hit Behance or It's Nice That yet, or (b) 2–5 year-old work being re-canonized by designers as reference. This catalog favors blocks ADDED to public channels in 2024-2026, focusing on works with identifiable designers and source URLs.*

## Featured works

### Koby Levy (The League) — The Ark, Jerusalem Design Week, 2024

- **URL (block)**: https://www.are.na/block/42177702
- **Featured by / source**: Are.na block added by Nir Tober (@nir-tober) on 29 December 2025 to the `posters` channel; source https://www.instagram.com/p/DATkqwMPHgv/
- **Country**: Israel (Jerusalem)
- **Medium**: print — festival identity poster
- **Format**: large-scale event poster, exhibition signage
- **Palette**: warm Jerusalem stone palette — #E8DCC3 limestone, #7E3F2C terracotta, #1A1512 basalt ink, #D5A22F golden-hour, one contrasting navy #1C2C4A. Biblical-light plus ink.
- **Composition**: Monumental Hebrew typography dominates; the ark motif abstracted into geometric frame containing the wordmark. Symmetrical, ceremonial, inheriting Israeli poster tradition (Tartakover / Ezer) with a looser hand.
- **Typography**: Hebrew display letterforms drawn for the piece; Latin transliteration set smaller in matched weight. Hebrew-first typographic hierarchy.
- **Trend signals**: dark-academia (ceremonial, warm-stone), editorial-maximalism (Hebrew-Latin dual-setting), variable-bold-typography (Hebrew display), solarpunk (warm-ceremonial optimism)
- **What's fresh**: continuing the tradition of Israeli typographic posters (Jerusalem Design Week has been a reliable showcase for this lineage) — post-2020 work that hasn't yet been catalogued in Western competition archives.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Hebrew-first hierarchy forces a Latin designer to rethink reading-order habits.
  - Ceremonial / festival identity justifies symmetrical monumentality — don't default-apologize into asymmetry.
  - Geographic stone palettes (Jerusalem limestone, Barcelona terracotta, Lisbon azulejo) beat generic "warm neutrals."
- **Pair with designer profiles**: peter-saville, paula-scher, kenya-hara

### Benoît Bodhuin — Timtam Type Specimen, 2025

- **URL (block)**: https://www.are.na/block/38548543
- **Featured by / source**: Are.na block added by Nir Tober on 4 August 2025; source https://www.typographicposters.com/benoit-bodhuin/686bc070afdced708b549f8d
- **Country**: France (Lille) — Benoît Bodhuin runs BBB Foundry
- **Medium**: print — type specimen poster
- **Format**: A1/A0 typographic specimen, possibly folded into a specimen booklet
- **Palette**: typically Bodhuin-signature — single saturated spot color (often #FF3D20 poppy red or #0018E7 ultramarine) on white/off-white. Two-color only.
- **Composition**: Specimen-as-artwork — the typeface's character set, weight variants, and unusual axes pushed to extremes all shown simultaneously. Letterforms behaving as sculptural objects, tightly nested, sometimes overlapping.
- **Typography**: Timtam (custom BBB Foundry face) itself is the subject — a quirky, playful-but-rigorous display face with unusual weight interpolations.
- **Trend signals**: variable-bold-typography (interpolation pushed), anti-design-brutalism (raw specimen posture), editorial-maximalism (density of character-set display), acid-graphics (single saturated color on white)
- **What's fresh**: type-specimen-as-poster continues to be the most reliable format for rigorous typographic work, and Bodhuin is arguably the most consistent practitioner of it since 2015 — his 2025 Timtam specimen is a worth-studying example.
- **What it teaches**:
  - A specimen is a poster. Treat it as a finished piece, not a sales collateral.
  - Two colors — one saturated, one paper — is sufficient for a specimen when the type carries the tension.
  - Nest letterforms into dense compositions rather than laying them out in rows; density IS the specimen's argument.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: massimo-vignelli, paula-scher, stefan-sagmeister

### Janice Beck — "Am I?" (100 beste Plakate 2023), re-canonized 2024

- **URL (block)**: https://www.are.na/block/32452475
- **Featured by / source**: Are.na block added by Nir Tober on 23 November 2024; source https://www.typographicposters.com/100besteplakate/664dea82060748d5390e01b4
- **Country**: Germany / Switzerland — 100 beste Plakate 2023 selection
- **Medium**: print — typographic poster
- **Format**: A1 poster, 100 beste Plakate selection
- **Palette**: high-contrast — saturated ground (often #FF3BA0 hot pink or #FFEC00 caution yellow) against black type; minimal supporting color.
- **Composition**: Existential question "Am I?" set at display scale with letterforms arranged asymmetrically; negative space carries as much weight as the letters. Characteristic 100BP rigor.
- **Typography**: custom or modified display face, extreme size contrast between hero and small body.
- **Trend signals**: variable-bold-typography, editorial-maximalism, anti-design-brutalism, acid-graphics (saturated grounds)
- **What's fresh**: re-canonization in 2024 — Are.na curators are pulling recent 100 beste Plakate selections into shared reference channels, accelerating the diffusion of Germanic typographic rigor into global practice.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Existential single-question headlines work at poster scale when the typography takes the weight.
  - 100 beste Plakate is a reliable pre-competition signal — works there in 2023 often surface in global channels in 2024-2025.
  - Single saturated ground color + black display type is the baseline that any 2026 typographic poster should beat.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: paula-scher, stefan-sagmeister, massimo-vignelli

### Kai Damian Matthiesen — Queer Underground Movement Night Party, 2023 (curated 2025)

- **URL (block)**: https://www.are.na/block/33393686
- **Featured by / source**: Are.na block added by Nir Tober on 4 January 2025; source https://www.typographicposters.com/kai-matthiesen/641dd6719e425c794efa5172
- **Country**: Switzerland (Bern) — Gaskessel Bern drag + variety party series
- **Medium**: print poster series
- **Format**: A2 music/event posters
- **Palette**: full-rainbow circus typography palette — #E12E27 red, #F5A623 orange, #FFDD00 yellow, #1DB954 green, #1480E8 blue, #7A3DA5 purple, all on white paper. Rainbow as manifesto.
- **Composition**: "Breaks with any convention of typography and hierarchy" — letterforms stack, overlap, twist. Each color applied to a different letter of the headline; mix-and-match fonts across one line.
- **Typography**: 4–6 typefaces per poster, mixed from historical circus / fairground signage; each letter its own decision.
- **Trend signals**: anti-design-brutalism (convention-breaking), acid-graphics (rainbow on saturated ground), editorial-maximalism, risograph (spot-color logic)
- **What's fresh**: explicit rejection of the "tasteful restraint" tendency in Swiss design — queer identity pushing toward circus-typography as a political statement.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Rainbow palettes work when assigned one-color-per-letter and no other decoration — not as a gradient.
  - Mixing 4+ typefaces per headline is allowed in event/party work; each letter gets its own foundry choice.
  - "Breaking hierarchy" only works if the convention is visible — this piece reads as convention-breaking BECAUSE it's Swiss.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: david-carson, stefan-sagmeister, paula-scher

### Mirko Borsche / Bureau Borsche — Nike Air Max 720 Poster, 2019 (re-canonized 2024)

- **URL (block)**: https://www.are.na/block/32452508
- **Featured by / source**: Are.na block added by Nir Tober on 23 November 2024; source https://searchsystem.co/post/190530952119/mirko-borsche-bureau-borsche-nike-space-capsule
- **Country**: Germany (Munich) — Bureau Borsche for Nike "Space Capsule"
- **Medium**: print + large-format sportswear campaign
- **Format**: A1/A0 retail and campaign posters
- **Palette**: signature Borsche — saturated gradient cyan-to-magenta #00E6FF → #FF00C8 with type in white or acid yellow #FFFD00. Airmax-sneaker energy.
- **Composition**: Product + overscaled one-word typography + gradient field — Bureau Borsche's signature three-layer stack. Product floats front, type punches through the middle, gradient radiates behind.
- **Typography**: custom display grotesk, high-contrast weight, often condensed for the hero word; small supporting type in disciplined grotesk.
- **Trend signals**: acid-graphics, variable-bold-typography, neo-futurism (gradient atmospheric), editorial-maximalism (product-first)
- **What's fresh**: re-canonization in 2024 — this 2019 Bureau Borsche work is being added to contemporary reference channels as the template that other sportswear campaigns are now chasing.
- **What it teaches**:
  - The three-layer product/type/gradient stack is a repeatable sportswear template; learn it before you reject it.
  - Gradients from cyan to magenta are the "acid" baseline; stay inside that axis or commit fully to a warmer axis (orange-to-pink).
  - One-word hero + product + gradient field = poster. Don't add more.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: james-white-signalnoise, peter-saville, stefan-sagmeister

### Elias Hanzer (Klasse Hickmann) — Rundgangsplakat UdK Berlin, 2017 (curated 2025)

- **URL (block)**: https://www.are.na/block/33393288
- **Featured by / source**: Are.na block added by Nir Tober on 4 January 2025; source https://www.typographicposters.com/klassehickmann/6449271c3f42b0767012929e
- **Country**: Germany (Berlin) — UdK Rundgang (annual university open-studios)
- **Medium**: print poster + animated GIF variant
- **Format**: A1 university-open-studios poster
- **Palette**: Berlin poster palette — #000000 ink, #FFFFFF paper, one hot accent (often #FF2020 alarm red or #0A3DFF ultramarine). Two-color + paper.
- **Composition**: Classic Klasse Hickmann education — typographic structure as hero, letterforms modulating in weight or width; animation extends the poster into motion on the GIF variant.
- **Typography**: grotesk display set at monumental scale, variable-weight or width modulation carries the visual interest; body text in small mono or grotesk.
- **Trend signals**: variable-bold-typography, editorial-maximalism (typography takes 80%+), risograph (spot-color + paper), neo-futurism (motion-still hybrid)
- **What's fresh**: Klasse Hickmann's pedagogy has been the single most-cited design-school influence on 2024–2026 typographic posters — student work from this class continues to be pulled into global reference.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Variable-weight modulation IS the idea; don't add a second idea on top.
  - A static poster with a motion variant (GIF) is a 2020s standard — plan both from the start.
  - Spot-color + paper + one typographic event = a Klasse Hickmann-shaped poster; use as a baseline, deviate deliberately.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: peter-saville, paula-scher, massimo-vignelli

## Patterns observed across these entries

1. **Are.na curators cite, they don't create.** Every block in this file was collected by a curator (Nir Tober, Haley, Luke Levisohn, etc.) — Are.na's value is the cross-cultural citation graph, not original work. Treat it as a reference index for work that's happening on the ground globally.
2. **Curators specialize by region or discipline.** Nir Tober owns the Hebrew graphic design / Jerusalem poster thread. Other curators own Korean, Latin American, or type-specimen threads. Follow the curator, not the tag.
3. **Source-provider bias: typographicposters.com, Instagram, searchsystem.co.** When tracing real designers from Are.na blocks, these three upstream sources account for roughly half the external links. Following those sites directly is often a shortcut past Are.na.
4. **Re-canonization is continuous.** The Bureau Borsche 2019 Nike piece being pulled into 2024 Are.na channels illustrates the lag: work becomes "reference" roughly 4–6 years after it ships. Anything added to an active channel in 2024-2026 will be canonical by 2028.
5. **Hebrew, Korean, and Latin-American typographic traditions are the three under-canonized currents.** Nearly every surveyed Are.na channel surfaces at least one strong piece from these traditions; competition archives (D&AD, Brno, Tokyo TDC) under-represent them.

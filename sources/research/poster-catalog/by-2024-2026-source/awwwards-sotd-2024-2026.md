# Awwwards Site of the Day: Works 2024-2026

*Awwwards is the dominant web-first awards platform; its Site of the Day (SOTD) jury scores 7.00+ sites across Design / Usability / Creativity / Content. While officially about websites, the hero-screen decisions on these sites are functionally poster decisions — a single full-bleed moment, one hero typographic idea, one palette, one compositional structure. The SOTDs that translate back to print are usually portfolio sites, brand-launch microsites, or spatial/architectural identity sites. This catalog selects eight such pieces from April 2026 back through 2025.*

## Featured works

### Lusion — Oryzo AI (cork-coaster product site), 2026

- **URL**: https://www.awwwards.com/sites/oryzo-ai
- **Featured by / source**: Awwwards SOTD 13 April 2026 (score 7.86, Developer Award + Site of the Day)
- **Country**: UK (Lusion, London)
- **Medium**: web (Three.js + WebGL + GSAP), hero-translatable to print
- **Format**: full-viewport landing, vertical scroll narrative
- **Palette**: #100904 deep bitumen-black, #FF8539 vibrant warm orange. Two colors only.
- **Composition**: Cinematic product story — a cork coaster stages 3D-to-2D transitions, WebGL sketches, gallery interstitials, and particle effects. Hero moment is a single object in deep space, orange light raking across. At poster scale: one object, one light, one warm accent on near-black.
- **Typography**: sans-serif grotesk held calm — type never competes with the object; set at medium weight, generous leading, one large headline + one small caption only.
- **Trend signals**: neo-futurism (spatial UI depth), aurora-mesh (atmospheric warm-light gradient), variable-bold-typography (restrained), editorial-maximalism (the object is the maximal move, not the type)
- **What's fresh**: a B2C CPG product (coaster) treated with the reverence of luxury car cinematography — genre-collision done straight.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Two colors is enough if one is near-black and the other is saturated enough to carry emotional temperature alone.
  - A single 3D hero object with raking light and generous negative space maps perfectly to poster format.
  - Don't caption the hero with marketing copy; use a small typographic counter (date, code, dimension) to ground the image.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: refik-anadol, james-white-signalnoise, kenya-hara

### Adoratorio Studio — Max Mara "Jacket Circle" interactive experience, 2026

- **URL**: https://www.awwwards.com/sites/max-mara-jacket-circle
- **Featured by / source**: Awwwards SOTD 16 April 2026 (Developer Award + Site of the Day)
- **Country**: Italy (Milan, Adoratorio Studio)
- **Medium**: web (HTML5 + GSAP + PixiJS) — translates to luxury-campaign print
- **Format**: interactive "imaginary words" game experience, hero full-bleed imagery
- **Palette**: warm coral/rose #E59291, soft golden-tan #E7D497, cream base, accents of deep burgundy. Rosé-gold atmosphere.
- **Composition**: Big background imagery + 3D wardrobe elements + microinteractions — hero reads as a single styled still-life, illustration layers floating at depth. Poster translation: one hero portrait, two illustration overlays, one wordmark.
- **Typography**: editorial serif for the hero wordmark / game concept words; small grotesk navigation captions. Serif carries luxury, grotesk carries function.
- **Trend signals**: editorial-maximalism, dark-academia (serif revival in luxury context), aurora-mesh (soft coral-to-tan gradients), dreamcore-liminal (peachy haze)
- **What's fresh**: luxury brand experience that's a game (not a shop) — narrative as the primary brand vehicle instead of photography-as-catalog.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Coral + golden-tan as the base luxury palette in 2026 replaces the beige + black that dominated 2018–2022.
  - A typography-led "imaginary word" headline can carry a campaign further than a photograph.
  - Illustration layered over photography at 30–40% opacity is the 2026 answer to pure photography OR pure illustration.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: peter-saville, paula-scher, kenya-hara

- **Featured by / source**: Awwwards SOTD 11 April 2026 (score 7.55)
- **Country**: Malvah — international PRO studio; Geoff Dawes collab (Australia)
- **Medium**: web (Contentful + GSAP + Three.js + BARBA.js), e-commerce with editorial hero
- **Format**: scroll-story landing with product pages
- **Palette**: pure white #FFFFFF + electric blue #050FFF. Two-tone only.
- **Composition**: Golf-as-ritual editorial treatment — hero moments treat apparel as subcultural artifact. Clean minimal layout with big-type interludes between product sections. Poster scale: white ground, one large blue word, one small photograph.
- **Typography**: oversized geometric display (brand-custom or Söhne-adjacent) in electric blue; body in small grotesk; display dominates composition.
- **Trend signals**: anti-design-brutalism (stark two-color), variable-bold-typography, bento-grid (product tile logic), acid-graphics (the saturation of #050FFF reads "digital, not Pantone")
- **What's fresh**: a merch brand that writes manifestos — treats golf apparel like a zine would, not like a lookbook.
- **What it teaches**:
  - #050FFF + pure white is a committing choice — the blue is not navy, not royal, it's screen-blue and must own that.
  - Subculture brands are allowed large editorial type moments between product; adopt this rhythm.
  - Two-tone works if one tone is deliberately screen-native (not Pantone-match).

## Patterns observed across these entries

1. **Two-color discipline.** Six of eight entries commit to a two-color palette (Oryzo's ink+orange, Odd Ritual's white+electric blue, Ravi Klaassens' burgundy+paper). The SOTDs that scored highest are the most chromatically restrained. Trend-wise, 2026 Awwwards is tightening color budgets compared to the rainbow-gradient 2021–2023 era.
2. **Hero object + raking light = poster moment.** Oryzo, MERSI, and Max Mara all stage a single object (coaster, building, jacket) with directional light. This translates directly to print: one subject, one illumination axis, one palette.
3. **Type > photography in the hero.** Ravi Klaassens, Studio X, Odd Ritual, and NaughtyDuk all hero with typography rather than photography. When photography leads (Evolve, MERSI), it's still treated editorially, not catalog-style.
4. **Variable-axis display faces are default.** Every studio-portfolio SOTD in this set uses either a commissioned variable face or a commercial variable face (Söhne, GT Flexa, ABC Diatype). Single-weight grotesks are now a tell that a site is older or undersigned.
5. **Motion-native capture.** All eight were motion-first designs; the SOTD thumbnail (the Awwwards jury sees a still) maps perfectly to a poster still. Motion rehearses the best poster frame for you.

# It's Nice That: Works 2024-2026

*It's Nice That has been the UK's leading design-press tastemaker since 2007; through its "Discover," "Articles," and "Features" streams it surfaces emerging studios and one-off projects 12–18 months before they appear in the D&AD / Brno / Chaumont archives. The editorial voice is consistently interested in studios that fuse rigorous typographic craft with personality, research, or regional specificity — a useful counterweight to Behance's volume-driven trending.*

## Featured works

### Nao Lee — "Becoming Ashes" (Jiun Koo solo exhibition poster), 2025

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/nao-lee-graphic-design-discover-160426
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "Nao Lee's posters are digital terrains that hide small and unruly treasures for you to find", 16 April 2026
- **Country**: South Korea (Seoul)
- **Medium**: digital, output to print
- **Format**: exhibition poster (portrait, approx B1/B2 print)
- **Palette**: #2A2215 warm near-black, #C9B892 aged-parchment cream, #8D5A3B earth umber, #D7502C vermilion accent, #6E6458 cold grey. Feels dug-up, archaeological.
- **Composition**: Layered digital noise forms a "map terrain" ground, refined illustration and typography sit above it; objects and letterforms half-hide inside patterned fields so the eye has to hunt. Vernacular packaging logics re-used as compositional devices.
- **Typography**: custom lettering and pulled-from-vernacular wordmarks; pairs with a neutral Hangul + Latin grotesk at body size; deliberately mismatched scale jumps.
- **Trend signals**: editorial-maximalism, dreamcore-liminal (nostalgic cast), risograph (grain surface), anti-design-brutalism (vernacular-packaging posture)
- **What's fresh**: noise-as-terrain rather than noise-as-decoration — the poster reads like a map with a narrative buried in it, not a flat background with grain on top.
- **What it teaches**:
  - A poster can be a search object; reward reward the 30-second stare.
  - Vernacular-packaging references beat design-history references for feeling 2026-contemporary.
  - Multiple small text events distributed across the field replace one hero headline.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: kenya-hara (for the quiet hand), josan-gonzalez (for the density discipline), paula-scher (for the vernacular cue reuse)

### Bravas Graphix (Paul Peyrolle & Jules Rousselet) — "Paris Freestyle Mixtape", 2025

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/bravas-graphix-paul-peyrolle-jules-rousselet-graphic-design-discover-020426
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "Bravas Graphix's explosive posters and stickers strike a balance between the punk and the academic", 2 April 2026
- **Country**: Belgium (Brussels)
- **Medium**: print — acetone transfer + photocopy + scan, riso-adjacent
- **Format**: A2 / A1 music posters, sticker pack, beer mats
- **Palette**: #B3FF1F radioactive green, #FF00A8 hot magenta, #05030A bitumen black, #F2EEDC off-white paper, #00D8FF digital cyan. Loud on purpose.
- **Composition**: Collaged scrap (flowers, coins, cut stickers) pasted into a broken academic grid; hierarchy obeyed just enough to be legible, broken just enough to feel alive. Composition as argument between order and spontaneity.
- **Typography**: grotesk wordmarks cut and re-laminated; display letters sometimes traced from archival matchbooks; mixed across 3+ cultures (French, English, nonsense-glyph).
- **Trend signals**: anti-design-brutalism, risograph, editorial-maximalism, acid-graphics (in the green)
- **What's fresh**: a principled reaction to Canva/AI homogenization — the tactile analog method IS the brand position, not nostalgia.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Use radioactive greens and hot magentas as the FIGURE on a calm paper ground, never both as figure.
  - Collage discipline: 3 archive fragments max, one contemporary cut, one hand mark. Stop.
  - Register music-poster tradition: borrowing / remixing / quoting is allowed and expected.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: stefan-sagmeister, david-carson, paula-scher

### Alexis Mark — Cobe architecture rebrand (identity + web), 2026

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/alexis-mark-cobe-branding-graphic-design-project-130426
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "This collaged architect's identity from Alexis Mark drew from departure boards and timetables", 13 April 2026
- **Country**: Denmark (Copenhagen) — studio co-founded by Marie Grønkær, Kristoffer Li, Martin Bek
- **Medium**: digital-first identity with print counterparts
- **Format**: website, print collateral, animated ticker components, stationery
- **Palette**: #0E0E0C near-black, #EFEDE6 paper warm-white, #FF5A14 safety-orange accent, #7A7773 industrial concrete grey, #193B2A forest (sparingly). Timetable / industrial signage.
- **Composition**: Layered collage quoting architectural blueprints — drawings, textures, and imagery overlap to embody Cobe's sustainable practice of re-using fragments. Layout borrows from transit terminals: departure boards, timetables, clipboards. Breaking-news tickers drive the motion layer.
- **Typography**: AM Kosmos (custom sans-serif by Alexis Mark) — slightly industrial, squarish counters, high x-height for tight setting. Pairs with Monospaced data labels.
- **Trend signals**: bento-grid (timetable modularity), variable-bold-typography (AM Kosmos hierarchy), neo-futurism (departure-board UI lineage), editorial-maximalism (overlap layers)
- **What's fresh**: an architect's brand that reads like Schiphol at 06:00 instead of like a minimalist wellness app — lets urgency be part of the voice.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Custom typeface is the backbone; everything else — collage, color, motion — plugs into it.
  - Reference transit systems if you want "systematic but alive," not "corporate sterile."
  - Motion tickers are underused in static poster work — a single moving strip changes the weight of an entire composition.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: massimo-vignelli, kenya-hara, peter-saville

### Jump Jirakaweekul — metallic-tattoo-flash poster series, 2025

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/jump-jirakaweekul-graphic-design-illustration-discover-070426
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "The metallic graphics of Jump Jirakaweekul are rooted in the ancient but feel sharp and current", 7 April 2026
- **Country**: USA / Thailand (New York-based)
- **Medium**: digital, vector + 3D metallic renders
- **Format**: poster set, flash-card-style prints, client work (Twitch w/ Collins)
- **Palette**: #1C1C1E obsidian, #9A9A9E polished chrome, #D4AF37 warm gold, #E52E2E caution red, #16C7F2 signage cyan. Metal + warning.
- **Composition**: Tattoo-flash grid logic — multiple discrete iconic objects (barbed wire, horned tails, spirals, CCTV cameras, dragons) arranged on a dark field. Each object feels embossed into concrete. Organic vs synthetic kept in visible tension.
- **Typography**: vector display letterforms shaped like signage glyphs — highway-marker proportions, soft blur embossing, occasional pictogram substitution for letters.
- **Trend signals**: acid-graphics (chrome), glitch (iconography distortion), neo-futurism (metallic depth), anti-design-brutalism (signage posture)
- **What's fresh**: 1990s rave and mythology braided together without nostalgia — neither Y2K pastiche nor heritage project.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Embossed chrome works when paired with ANCIENT iconography (not with tech-bro branding).
  - Tattoo-flash composition (multiple icons, no single hero) is a viable poster structure if the icons carry enough tension.
  - Combine synthetic and organic objects deliberately; the tension IS the piece.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: james-white-signalnoise, ash-thorp, josan-gonzalez

### Little Troop — Bon Elliot skincare identity, 2026

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/little-troop-bon-elliot-branding-graphic-design-project-150426
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "Meet Bon Elliot: the family-run, slow-crafted skincare label from Little Troop", 15 April 2026
- **Country**: USA (New York studio) / Australia (client Sydney)
- **Medium**: print + digital identity, campaign photography
- **Format**: bottles, packaging, campaign posters, look book
- **Palette**: warm family of neutrals — #EDE6D8 sand, #C9B79F biscuit, #8A7A66 taupe, #26211B bitter chocolate, soft-light transparencies between.
- **Composition**: Symmetrical mark (B+E converged) behaves as an animatable "infographic of the three skin layers." Campaign compositions quote Irving Penn's minimalist still-life and Kubrick's axial symmetry.
- **Typography**: Avant Garde, stacked all-caps — geometric, calm, luxury posture.
- **Trend signals**: bento-grid (infographic mark), neo-futurism (clinical luxury), dark-academia (warm restraint), dreamcore-liminal (Kodak-cast campaign photography)
- **What's fresh**: symmetry-as-editorial, borrowed from 1960s Penn but with a system mindset — the mark IS the data diagram.
- **What it teaches**:
  - A wordmark that moonlights as a diagram outperforms a standalone lockup.
  - Irving Penn + Kubrick is a defensible reference stack for any "quiet luxury" brief.
  - Warm neutrals beat cool neutrals if the product is skincare / slow-crafted / family.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: kenya-hara, peter-saville, massimo-vignelli

### Blurr Bureau — Yes! Apples packaging + campaign, 2026

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/blurr-bureau-yes-apples-branding-graphic-design-project-080426
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "Blurr Bureau gives us a lesson in how to brand something that's been around forever: Apples", 8 April 2026
- **Country**: USA (New York) — client Yes! Apples cooperative of NY family-run orchards
- **Medium**: print packaging, produce stickers, posters, in-store
- **Format**: fruit sticker system, crates, promotional posters
- **Palette**: market-sign color-coded system — #D8221F apple red, #F2C230 pippin yellow, #3C8A3A orchard green, #F8EED6 paper ivory, #10100E ink. Each variety owns a color.
- **Composition**: Logo reimagines Milton Glaser's "I love NY" with an apple at the core; the "Y" doubles as a tree sprouting from seed. Sticker-style labels carry flavor + nutrition as copy-centric typographic blocks.
- **Typography**: Perfekta (Displaay) paired with Exposure (2025TF) — modern rounded body-setting that echoes apple roundness + editorial subhead grotesk.
- **Trend signals**: editorial-maximalism (sticker overload), risograph (flat-color market-sign lineage), variable-bold-typography (hero wordmark weights), anti-design-brutalism (earnest vernacular)
- **What's fresh**: self-aware homage (Glaser's IHNY) that re-earns the reference via a functional system (varietal color codes), not a wink.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Vernacular sticker logic is a 2026-valid poster structure if the variants are the point.
  - Quoting a canonical logo only works when you also SUBTRACT something (in this case: the verbal tagline) and ADD a system (color-coded varieties).
  - Pair a rounded grotesk with a sharper subhead grotesk — rounded alone reads juvenile, sharper alone reads corporate.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: paula-scher, stefan-sagmeister, kenya-hara

### Crown Creative — Barkhouse dog-hotel identity, 2026

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/crown-creative-barkhouse-illustration-graphic-design-project-300326
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "The loveable line drawings of this dog hotel brand identity take cues from The New Yorker's illustrations", 30 March 2026
- **Country**: UK (Belfast, Crown Creative) / client USA (New York)
- **Medium**: print + digital — identity, print magazine (Barkhouse Bulletin), object graphics
- **Format**: signage, fetch balls, full newspaper publication, website
- **Palette**: cream + warm ochre + ink-navy base; muted saturated accents per dog portrait. #F3EBDA parchment, #1E2A3C blue-black ink, #C6873B hazelnut, #7C9A6C moss, #B93E33 oxblood.
- **Composition**: Portrait-led — each dog gets a specific observational line drawing at poster scale; typography behaves as caption, not headline.
- **Typography**: simultaneous serif + sans — the serif is the owner's voice, the sans is the dog's. Generous leading, editorial feel.
- **Trend signals**: dark-academia (editorial serif + warm paper), editorial-maximalism (portrait + caption treatment), risograph (imperfect linework)
- **What's fresh**: treats the pet-care category like The New Yorker treats a cover — observational, character-specific, unhurried.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Pairing a serif and a sans as two voices is a brief-ready concept, not a compromise.
  - Line-drawing imperfection signals warmth; vector-perfect linework signals corporate.
  - A brand that includes a full newspaper ("Barkhouse Bulletin") has infinite poster surface baked in.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: paula-scher, kenya-hara, peter-saville

### Xander Opiyo & Gala Mendoza — Third Place Zine, Issue 2 "Movement Building", 2025

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/third-place-zine-xander-opiyo-gala-mendoza-publication-project-310326
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "'Not too design-y': Third Place Zine is a playful publication about city life that's built for everyone", 31 March 2026
- **Country**: UK
- **Medium**: print zine — collage + hand-drawing + photography
- **Format**: saddle-stitched zine, accompanying launch posters, stockists worldwide
- **Palette**: shifts per issue; Issue 2 leans cyan + peach + newsprint black. #EDE5D8 newsprint, #00A0B4 cyan, #E98A6E peach, #171514 ink, occasional #FF443B hot red.
- **Composition**: "Not too design-y" — visible decisions are collage-first, hand-drawn overlays on photographs, type as cut-out rather than set. Hierarchy relaxed.
- **Typography**: Arial Narrow as hero — self-aware choice of "the most designer-despised typeface" re-elevated by tight setting and generous surrounding space.
- **Trend signals**: anti-design-brutalism, risograph, editorial-maximalism, dreamcore-liminal (cyan-peach haze)
- **What's fresh**: Arial Narrow as a deliberate signature — this is the 2026 version of David Carson's Ray Gun Helvetica subversion.
- **What it teaches**:
  - An "ugly" system typeface in the hero slot can be a strong move if the surrounding craft is tight.
  - Accessibility-as-aesthetic — building "not too design-y" into the brief yields warmer work than polish-first briefs.
  - Zine format forces decisions that poster designers should borrow: cut-out type, hand-drawn overlays, unafraid hierarchy.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: david-carson, paula-scher, stefan-sagmeister

### Risotto Studio (Gabriella Marcella et al) — Riso Club 100 (Risotopia), 2026

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/features/risotto-riso-club-graphic-design-spotlight-080426
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, feature "The joy and power of Risograph: Risotto showcases 400 printed postcards from artists across the globe", 8 April 2026
- **Country**: Scotland (Glasgow) — Risotto Studio; contributors international (Peter Shire, Nathalie Du Pasquier, Barbara Stauffacher Solomon, Anthony Burrill, Raissa Pardini, Marina Willer / Pentagram)
- **Medium**: risograph print
- **Format**: 400 postcards installed as grid at The Glue Factory, 11–19 April 2026; digital atlas
- **Palette**: 2–3 riso inks per card — fluorescent pink #FF4998, teal #00A0B4, federal blue #2E3798, yellow #FFD21F, black; registration by design slightly off.
- **Composition**: grid-as-exhibition — 400 individual postcards read as one hero piece at gallery distance; each card a self-contained composition. At poster scale, a single card's decisions read large.
- **Typography**: contributor-specific; across the set, hand-drawn lettering and geometric grotesks dominate; Stauffacher Solomon's Supergraphics lineage visible.
- **Trend signals**: risograph, editorial-maximalism (grid-as-piece), solarpunk (hand-crafted, hopeful), variable-bold-typography
- **What's fresh**: monthly-delivered editorial + 100-month grid as a single "piece" — the system is the artwork, not the card.
- **What it teaches**:
  - 2 colors + paper grain can carry more visual weight than 5-color CMYK when the ink variance is embraced.
  - A monthly edition is a practical brief: one risograph postcard, two inks, 148×105mm, one idea.
  - Group exhibitions where 400 individual pieces read as one installation can be replicated at poster-series scale (quarterly drop of 4–8 posters sharing one ink system).
- **Pair with designer profiles**: paula-scher, stefan-sagmeister, josan-gonzalez

### Oilinwater (Matthieu Gorissen) — Maison Hannon museum identity, 2025

- **URL**: https://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/oilinwater-graphic-design-discover-sponsored-content-310326
- **Featured by / source**: It's Nice That, article "Oilinwater approaches branding like an investigator, to build visual systems from rigorous research", 31 March 2026
- **Country**: Belgium (Brussels)
- **Medium**: print + digital — identity, signage, print ephemera
- **Format**: museum identity system (Maison Hannon, an Art Nouveau townhouse museum); related projects Picturesque exhibition, Styx cinema, Centre Culturel d'Uccle
- **Palette**: aged-paper warm + jewel secondary — #EDE4D2 aged vellum, #24281D forest ink, #8E2E28 oxblood, #AF7A3C Hannon brass, soft grey-green #6E8572.
- **Composition**: Wordmark derived from the building's own door-handle curve — research-as-form. Signage and print compositions leave the research visible (footnotes, citations, archival fragments alongside the main mark).
- **Typography**: Art Nouveau-adjacent custom drawn curves for display; clean transitional serif (Freight-ish) for body; matched kerning between the hand-drawn display and the typeset body.
- **Trend signals**: dark-academia (research-visible, warm paper), solarpunk (art-nouveau revival, optimistic), editorial-maximalism (citation apparatus used as ornament)
- **What's fresh**: door-handle-as-wordmark is a research-method signature — every brand gets its own derived form, nothing off-the-shelf.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Derive at least one graphic element from a physical artifact on site — a door handle, a floor tile, a manhole. Never skip this step for identity briefs.
  - Keep the research apparatus (sources, citations, sketches) in the final deliverable at small size; it teaches the viewer how to read the work.
  - Art Nouveau references work when paired with transitional serifs and restrained palettes; they collapse when paired with modern grotesks.
- **Pair with designer profiles**: peter-saville, kenya-hara, massimo-vignelli

## Patterns observed across these entries

1. **Custom typeface or hand-drawn display, always.** Eight of ten entries have either a commissioned custom face (AM Kosmos, Nao Lee's Hangul display, Oilinwater's door-handle wordmark) or a deliberately chosen "signature" typeface (Arial Narrow in Third Place Zine, Avant Garde in Bon Elliot). The neutral-grotesk-as-default posture that dominated 2018–2022 has vanished from this editorial.
2. **Research or physical-artifact derivation.** Work that passes the editorial filter usually derives a core graphic element from a research artifact — a door handle, a departure board, a fruit sticker, an apple-orchard market sign. Decorative flourish without provenance reads as styled rather than designed.
3. **Collage and tactile print vs. clean digital, 50/50.** The editorial doesn't favor one mode; Bravas Graphix (tactile collage) and Alexis Mark (system digital) get equal coverage. What's consistent is that the chosen mode is committed to — hybrids read as un-decided.
4. **Regional specificity over global homogeneity.** Seoul, Brussels, Copenhagen, Sydney, New York, Belfast, Glasgow — the editorial rewards work that feels situated. Studios that read "international" without a located voice tend not to clear the discover bar.
5. **Portraits, sticker logics, and ticker-style data graphics are the three compositional structures recurring.** A portrait-led layout (Barkhouse, Nao Lee), a sticker/variant logic (Yes! Apples, Riso Club), or a timetable/ticker (Cobe, departure-board implied) appears in almost every recent "Discover" feature.

# 2024–2026 — Contemporary Poster Catalog

*The most load-bearing file in the era catalog. What is winning NOW — D&AD Black Pencils 2024, Tokyo TDC 2025 Grand Prix, AIGA 50/50 2024, and the brand-identity systems shipped by Pentagram, Studio Dumbar, Porto Rocha, Underware, Sagmeister and their peers since January 2024. The agent should pull from this file first when "do something that feels 2026" is the brief.*

## Context

Three shifts define these three years. First, the anti-AI craft counter-movement: after two years of Midjourney floods, the prestige competitions (D&AD, Tokyo TDC, AIGA 50/50) started visibly rewarding visible-hand work — hand-set type, risograph, custom lettering, bespoke typefaces. Second, identity-as-system maturity: Porto Rocha's Robinhood, Studio Dumbar's Transavia, Kontrapunkt's FC København, Nejc Prah's Center Rog all shipped "loose system" identities — predefined triads/palettes plus intentional inconsistency, the opposite of a rigid brand manual. Third, the end of the one-style-per-brand era: Spotify Wrapped 2022 proved a brand can splinter into 48 looks; by 2024, the Center Rog identity formalized intentional-eclecticism into a TDC-prize-winning methodology.

The 2026 continuation point: Futura's centennial (Tokyo TDC 2026 Grand Prix to Veronika Burian & José Scaglione for Futura®100 Multiscript) signals typography scholarship coming back into the award spotlight. Spatial UI post-Vision Pro (launched Feb 2, 2024) is the landing-page texture now — depth, glass, and soft shadow, with the bento grid as floor plan.

## Canonical works

### Dentsu Tokyo — "My Japan Railway" / JR Group, 2024

- **URL**: https://www.dandad.org/awards/professional/2024/238082/my-japan-railway/
- **Source competition / publication**: D&AD 2024 Black Pencil in Art Direction; plus 70+ other awards including One Show Grand Prix, Clio Awards, London International Awards
- **Country / city**: Japan / Tokyo (Dentsu Inc.) with Creative Power Unit + Adbrain as production
- **Medium**: Multi-channel — app + digital stamps + printed station posters + public signage
- **Format**: Digital stamp units ~750×750 px; OOH adaptations to platform signage
- **Palette**: Regional-variable — the system issues a custom color palette per JR region (hokuriku amethyst, kyushu volcanic black, shikoku sea-blue, etc.), but a shared poster format holds all 4,000+ stations under one visual rule. Common anchors: #E53935 JR red, #1A1A1A ink black, #F5F1E8 washi cream.
- **Composition**: Each poster is a single circular stamp — station icon center, station name katakana + romaji stacked below. Pressed-ink imperfection preserved deliberately. Posters tile into a prefecture grid at the regional level. The eye lands on the stamp first, then the place name.
- **Typography**: Dentsu-commissioned custom Japanese family with English companion; letterforms use low-stroke-contrast grotesque for katakana and a refined humanist sans for Latin. Weight strategy: medium for name, semibold for region tag.
- **Trend signals**: bento-grid (gridded prefecture walls), dreamcore-liminal (nostalgic travel tone), risograph (hand-pressed ink feel), variable-bold-typography
- **What's fresh about it**: It's a campaign disguised as infrastructure. 1.8M app users, 11M stamps pressed in 30 months. The poster here *is* the product — not a promotion of one.
- **What it teaches**:
  - A poster-as-interaction beats a poster-as-announcement
  - Hand-press imperfection, kept as a signature, is a craft-anchoring move
  - One-format-many-colors + regional palette scales nationwide without fracture
- **Pair with designer profiles**: kenya-hara, massimo-vignelli, paula-scher
- **Pair with briefs**: transit identity, tourism brands, infrastructure campaigns, stamp/postal series

### Apple Design Team — "visionOS" spatial-computing identity, 2024

- **URL**: https://www.apple.com/apple-vision-pro/
- **Source competition / publication**: D&AD 2024 Black Pencil in Digital Design; Apple Vision Pro launched February 2, 2024
- **Country / city**: USA / Cupertino
- **Medium**: Keynote slides + App Store key art + landing pages + OS marketing
- **Format**: 16:9 keynote, adapted to landing-page card grid; vertical spatial renders
- **Palette**: System palette — #FFFFFF / #000000 / #1D1D1F near-black / #007AFF system blue / transparent glass-gradient layers. Translucent layering is the real palette.
- **Composition**: Floating window panels stacked in perspective z-depth; content hovers over a blurred environment. Bento-grid persists but tilted into 3D space. Eye lands where shadows are softest and strongest — the depth signal is the hierarchy.
- **Typography**: SF Pro Display for headlines, variable-weight; SF Pro Rounded for glanceable UI micro-type. Weight treatment emphasizes readability at any focal distance, not just poster scale.
- **Trend signals**: neo-futurism, aurora-mesh (through glass blur), bento-grid, variable-bold-typography
- **What's fresh about it**: Apple ships a new texture — "glass that is aware of light" — which is now the dominant landing-page material for enterprise SaaS in 2025-2026. Soft shadow + refractive layering is the new flat.
- **What it teaches**:
  - Depth without dropshadow — use blur and transparency for hierarchy
  - Every window should cast a plausible shadow; wrong-angle shadow reads as amateur
  - Big type still works at z-distance; small type disappears
- **Pair with designer profiles**: peter-saville, massimo-vignelli, ash-thorp
- **Pair with briefs**: AI product launches, OS identity, future-tech keynotes, XR/AR app covers

### Baku Hashimoto (with Tezzo Suzuki, Nao Tokui) — "MONO NO AWARE / Kamukamo-Shikamo-Nidomokamo! (imai remix)", 2025

- **URL**: https://tokyotypedirectorsclub.org/en/award/2025_grandprix/
- **Source competition / publication**: Tokyo TDC 2025 Grand Prix (35th edition) — winning work across all categories
- **Country / city**: Japan / Tokyo
- **Medium**: Music video + printed poster set (frame-stills as gallery editions)
- **Format**: 4K vertical + horizontal for music video; A1 and B2 printed frame posters
- **Palette**: #FFFFFF paper ground / #0A0A0A deep-ink black / single-frame hot accents of #E9381C red, #2F5DBF cobalt, #D4B600 gold — pen-plotter colors, intentionally analog
- **Composition**: A single Unicode character per frame, drawn by pen plotter onto paper, then sorted by an AI-trained perceptual-similarity model. The frame is a near-full-bleed character centered on paper. Eye lands on the glyph with no competition.
- **Typography**: Every Unicode glyph ever assigned — thousands of them. The *work is the typography*. The credit type for exhibition captioning is Helvetica Now Display, in Japanese set in Iwata UD Mincho.
- **Trend signals**: anti-design-brutalism (hand-plotter craft vs. AI), risograph, variable-bold-typography, dark-academia
- **What's fresh about it**: Pen-plotter pace meets ML sort order. Every glyph is machine-selected but human-drawn. This is the exact answer the post-AI era was asking for: "visible craft on top of the machine."
- **What it teaches**:
  - When the dataset is the brief, let the sort order do the layout
  - Plotter imperfection is the signature of human time invested
  - One glyph per frame, at poster scale, is an entire campaign
- **Pair with designer profiles**: kenya-hara, david-carson, refik-anadol
- **Pair with briefs**: music-video art direction, type-specimen posters, ML-output exhibitions

### Paula Scher / Pentagram — The Public Theater ongoing identity, 2024–25 season

- **URL**: https://www.pentagram.com/work/the-public-theater
- **Source competition / publication**: Pentagram NYC portfolio; featured in Eye on Design, Creative Review retrospective coverage of the 30-year Scher–Public Theater relationship
- **Country / city**: USA / New York
- **Medium**: Poster series (Shakespeare in the Park plus main-stage season), banner OOH across Manhattan, subway domination, playbill system
- **Format**: One-sheet 27×40 standard; banners 4×16 ft; subway platform 4×6 ft
- **Palette**: Season-variable. 2024-25 leaned #FF3B30 vermillion / #161616 ink-black / #F4EBDE cream, with production-specific accents per play. Pastels recur for comedies, high-saturation for tragedies.
- **Composition**: Title dominates the composition at 60-80% of the poster height; play names are set in Knockout at scales that clip letter edges and bleed off the sheet. Eye lands wherever the letters are heaviest — typically the verb or the proper noun.
- **Typography**: Hoefler & Co. (now Monotype) Knockout family — Scher has used the family as the Public's visual equivalent of DNA since 1994. Each season changes mix + color, never the family. Weight range: thin condensed body to black extended title.
- **Trend signals**: editorial-maximalism, variable-bold-typography, anti-design-brutalism
- **What's fresh about it**: In a portfolio-by-recency culture, 30 years of disciplined type-family use remains the most aggressive brand argument. The "fresh" thing about it in 2024 is that it hasn't changed — and that restraint reads as *more* distinctive, not less.
- **What it teaches**:
  - A single type family, used for 30 years, becomes proprietary
  - Vary the color, not the typography, to mark each season
  - Bleed-off-edge composition reads as confidence at any scale
- **Pair with designer profiles**: paula-scher, massimo-vignelli, peter-saville
- **Pair with briefs**: theater seasons, film festivals, arts-organization rebrands, long-running identity systems

## Trends observed across these entries

1. **Typography becomes the brand's primary proprietary asset.** Kontrapunkt's FC København custom type, Underware's Flux, Porto Rocha's Nike monospace, Transwhite's CN-EN custom pair — the 2024-2026 marker of prestige brand work is commissioned typography. Off-the-shelf Helvetica is a small-budget tell.

2. **The anti-AI craft signature.** Hand-plotter (Hashimoto), screenprint rubbings (Willer), risograph texture (multiple AIGA selections), oil-paint inlays (Sagmeister), hand-set Knockout (Scher). Prestige juries in 2024-2025 reward visible human time invested, explicitly against the grain of generative-image output.

3. **Loose system > rigid manual.** Center Rog's 4-triad intentional-eclecticism, Spotify Wrapped's 48 monograms, Studio Dumbar's DEMO color-rotation, My Japan Railway's regional palettes. The 2024-2026 brand system embraces variability as a feature, with only 1-2 locked elements.

4. **Numbers as heroes.** Nike Run stats at 180-240pt, Robinhood's stock-chart posters, Sagmeister's data-inlaid paintings, Spotify Wrapped's typographic stat cards. The big typographic number is now the emotional payload in performance, finance, and wellness brands.

5. **Glass, depth, and bento grid matured.** Post-visionOS (Feb 2024), landing-page texture tilted toward z-depth — blurred glass panels, soft shadows, tilted bento cards. The flat 2015-2020 era is fully over in enterprise SaaS.

6. **Women's and subsidiary brands as equal siblings.** FC København's women's team uses the same type system with a different accent, not a pink downgrade. Marcel's WoMen's Football literalized the point with the ad itself. Equal-sibling design patterns are the new 2024-2026 structural move.

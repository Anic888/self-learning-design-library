# Kenya Hara — Style Profile

## Core Identity
Japanese modern minimalism grounded in philosophy, not fashion. **Emptiness as active material** (ma 間). Muji's visual voice. Photography of vast horizons + delicate typography + massive white fields. Closer to poetry than to design-system thinking.

## Palette
- `#FBF8F0` / `#F5F0E6` — warm white / off-white (dominant, 60-80%)
- `#C8BFA4` / `#D4C8A8` — kraft / unbleached natural tone (10-20%)
- `#0E0E0E` — near-black (text, 5-10%)
- Occasional muted earth: `#8B7355` (warm brown), `#6B7A6A` (muted green)
- **Extremely rare saturation** — when introduced, it's surgical (one red dot, one blue line)
- **Tendency:** dominated by whites and naturals. Multiple whites (warm vs cool, matte vs glossy) treated as distinct colors. Color is sparingly introduced for accent.

## Typography
- **Japanese + Latin pairing** — typical. Each script set in typefaces that speak to each other.
- **Horizontal Latin type:** humanist sans (Akzidenz-Grotesk, Helvetica Now, Muji custom)
- **Japanese vertical typesetting** (tategaki) when appropriate — top-to-bottom
- Light weights dominant — almost never bold
- **Generous line-height** (1.6-2.0) — breathing through type
- **Small point sizes** common — type doesn't shout
- **Generous margins** — type islands in white fields
- **Tracking neutral or slightly positive** — never tight

## Composition & Grid
- **Extreme whitespace** — 70-85% of composition can be "empty"
- **Horizon lines** as primary composition device — landscape photography cropped to flat horizons
- **Off-center subject placement** often — bottom-third or side-third
- **Asymmetric balance** — one dense area countered by large empty area
- **Rule of thirds respected gently** — not dogmatic
- **Modular grid** underneath but invisible to viewer

## Signature Moves
- **Horizon photography** — vast horizontal landscapes (Mongolian steppe, Uluru, Bolivian salt flats)
- **One small figure** in huge empty field — massive scale contrast
- **White as active material** — not residual, but felt
- **Multiple whites** in one piece — paper white, neutral white, warm white, natural linen
- **Paper/material texture** as content — embossing, debossing, kraft surfaces
- **Japanese character integrated** — single kanji or phrase in respectful dialogue with Latin type
- **Sensory emphasis** — his exhibitions emphasize touch/smell/hearing, visible in how work feels (paper choices, printing technique)
- **No imagery in some pieces** — just type in white field
- **Depth through subtle tonal variation** — whites interplaying

## Techniques / Tools
- InDesign / Illustrator (standard Western tooling)
- Physical paper expertise — specifies actual stock, printing technique
- Large-format photography (contracted — he art-directs, doesn't shoot)
- Hand-sketching / model-making for conceptual phase
- Works in collaboration with specialist typographers for Japanese forms

## Mood / Emotional Register
Contemplative, generous, patient. Viewer must slow down. "Breathing room" is a feature not a byproduct. Not cold (Swiss) nor warm-commercial (Scher) — **warm-quiet**. Emotional register of a Japanese tea room applied to commercial design.

## When to apply
- ✅ Lifestyle / wellness brands wanting "quiet premium" feel
- ✅ Book design (his book work is exemplary)
- ✅ Gallery/museum exhibition graphics
- ✅ Retail with thoughtful narrative (Muji tradition)
- ✅ When project needs to feel "less designed, more considered"
- ❌ NOT for loud commercial, sales-driven campaigns
- ❌ NOT for dense information (too much breathing room)
- ❌ NOT for youth-culture / rebellious contexts

## How to apply (tactical for Claude)
1. **Start with massive white space.** Content occupies 15-30% of composition.
2. **Palette: warm white + kraft + near-black + (optional) ONE muted earth accent.** No saturated color by default.
3. **Horizon line** across composition — landscape photo or implied through layout
4. **One small element** occupying tiny fraction of space (figure, word, object) — huge scale contrast with empty field
5. **Typography: light weight, humanist sans, generous leading and margins.** Never bold, never tight.
6. **Pair Japanese + Latin** when appropriate — set carefully, don't decorate with kanji
7. **Multiple "whites"** — suggest paper/material through subtle tonal shifts (slight cream vs slight cool white)
8. **Asymmetric composition** — dense corner, empty large area
9. **NO decorative elements** — no borders, no ornaments, no visual noise
10. **NOT to use:** saturated palettes, filled compositions, heavy typography, Western center-symmetry, gradient backgrounds. All violate the quiet.

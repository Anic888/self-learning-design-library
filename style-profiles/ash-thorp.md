# Ash Thorp — Style Profile

## Core Identity
Painterly atmospheric illustration with cinematic-UI heritage. Heads ALT Creative Inc. Half of work is UI/motion direction (BR2049 legacy), half is moody character/creature/environment illustration. Unifying constant: **atmospheric depth** — haze, volumetric light, sense of scale through moisture-filled air.

## Palette
Key insight: **each piece is monochromatic + 1 accent, but accent choice varies dramatically across his body of work.** Not a single "Thorp palette" — rather a **"Thorp method"** applied to any hue.

Recent observed palettes (verified Behance 2026-04):
- **Burnt orange + deep browns** — fire/ember pieces (dramatic silhouettes against flame)
- **Deep teal/cyan + near-black** — underwater/submerged compositions
- **Warm yellow/sepia + muted green** — desert ruins with atmospheric perspective
- **Hot pink/magenta + orange gradient** — synthwave-adjacent landscape
- **Cold grayscale + red** — dark fantasy/demon figures
- **Near-black + yellow glow point** — void compositions with single light source

**The rule:** one dominant hue family (70-85%), muted mid-tones (10-20%), ONE punchy accent (5-10%). Strictly enforced per piece. Black and off-white available as neutrals in any palette.

## Typography
- **Minimal in illustrations** — often title/text is separate (motion design credits)
- **UI-oriented work:** condensed geometric sans (Eurostile, Aktiv Grotesk Extended, GT America Mono)
- Uppercase with wide tracking (100-300 units)
- Light/thin weights dominate
- Text treated as data-readout or functional label, not emotional subject
- **Japanese katakana/kanji** sometimes as atmospheric addition in UI work (Ghost in the Shell era)

## Composition & Grid
- **Center-subject dominance** — single figure/object in composition center or slight off-center
- **Massive atmospheric void around subject** — 40-60% negative space filled with haze/gradient/fog
- **Horizon-anchored** when landscape-oriented
- **Layered planes through atmospheric perspective** — not explicit grid, but depth through opacity/desaturation of distant elements
- **Rule of thirds respected but not dogmatic**
- **3-5 visual planes** through haze separation

## Signature Moves
- **Atmospheric haze** as primary depth device (not blur, but opacity + desaturation of distant elements)
- **Volumetric light rays** — visible god-rays, dust particles in beams
- **Central silhouette vs bright background** OR **bright subject vs deep void**
- **Environmental moisture implied** — wet surfaces, mist, fog
- **One strong light source** — sun, fire, holographic point; everything else in relative shadow
- **Thin linework** for UI-specific work (0.4-1px strokes, never heavy)
- **HUD data-readouts** in corners when composition needs UI language
- **Moisture/mist particles** — visible in atmosphere
- **Dramatic scale contrast** — tiny human silhouette against huge subject/environment
- **Painterly finish** even on digital work — rendered, not vector-flat

## Mood / Emotional Register
Meditative, atmospheric, grand. "Witnessing something ancient or vast." Not gritty like cyberpunk, not cheerful like synthwave, not cold like Swiss modernism. **Reverent** — subject is presented as worthy of contemplation.

## When to apply
- ✅ Cinematic posters, book covers
- ✅ Atmospheric environment illustration
- ✅ Character/creature reveal art
- ✅ UI-driven compositions with gravitas
- ✅ When you want "cinematic single shot" energy
- ❌ NOT for party flyers, maximalist, playful
- ❌ NOT for flat-design / illustration without depth
- ❌ NOT for generic "cyberpunk neon" (this is Josan's zone, not Thorp's)

## How to apply (tactical for Claude)
1. **Pick ONE dominant hue family.** Everything else is neutrals/desaturated variants. Add ONE accent (often warm when base is cool, or vice versa).
2. **Central subject rule** — ONE dominant element, clearly focal. Let it breathe.
3. **Atmospheric haze is mandatory** — distant elements should be desaturated and slightly brighter (aerial perspective). Never pure sharp edges all the way to horizon.
4. **One strong light source** — sun, fire, holographic glow, reflected light. Everything lit from one direction + its bounce.
5. **Scale contrast** — if depicting environment, add tiny human figure or small detail for reference of massive subject.
6. **Volumetric light rays** when light source is visible in frame.
7. **If UI/motion work: thin strokes (0.4-1px), wide-tracked uppercase type, corner HUD readouts, horizon anchor.**
8. **If illustration work: painterly rendering with soft edges, atmospheric moisture, single dominant figure.**
9. **NO RGB split glitch, NO neon gradients, NO heavy solid blocks, NO scanlines as decorative.**

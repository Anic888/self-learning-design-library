# Massimo Vignelli — Style Profile

## Core Identity
**Swiss modernism at American commercial scale**. Six-typeface discipline (his rule: Bodoni, Garamond, Century Expanded, Futura, Helvetica, Times Roman — "you don't need more"). Grid-obsessive systems thinking. Form follows function, and function follows rigorous research. **The antidote to Carson.**

## Palette
- `#E20E17` / `#D91E26` — signature red (Knoll, various identities, 10-25%)
- `#0E0E0E` — pure-feeling black (dominant type, 25-40%)
- `#F5F5F0` / `#FFFFFF` — white ground (40-60%)
- Occasional: primary yellow `#FED102`, primary blue `#1E429F`
- **Rule: NEVER gradients.** Flat color only. Color as semantic, not decorative.
- **Restraint:** 2-3 colors max per identity system. Often only red/black/white.

## Typography
**The Vignelli Six (his stated discipline):**
- **Bodoni** — classical serif, editorial
- **Garamond** — traditional book serif
- **Century Expanded** — transitional serif
- **Futura** — geometric sans
- **Helvetica** — humanist sans (his most-used)
- **Times Roman** — news/editorial serif

Rules for all:
- Always crisp, clean, no effects
- **Tight but not touching** letter-spacing on headlines
- Neutral tracking on body
- Generous leading (1.4-1.6)
- Consistent grid alignment
- **No italicization for emphasis** — use weight instead
- Heavy weights rare; medium or regular dominant

## Composition & Grid
- **12-column or 16-column modular grid** — rigid, followed
- **Type hangs on baseline grid** — strict
- **Every element snaps to grid** — no freehand placement
- **Symmetric when content demands, asymmetric when content demands** — never arbitrary
- **Massive system thinking** — one identity system = hundreds of applications, all coherent
- **Whitespace as structural element** — generous margins, consistent
- **Headline + subhead + body hierarchy** crystal clear

## Signature Moves
- **Red-black-white palette** — iconic
- **Helvetica everywhere** (after 1960) — defining typeface
- **Diagrammatic simplification** — subway map as pure geometry, not geographic accuracy
- **Flat color, no gradients ever**
- **Bold horizontal rules** as structural dividers
- **Stacked bauhaus-style titling** — multiple sizes aligned left
- **Minimal visual noise** — every mark has a job
- **System over artifact** — any single piece is a sample of the system
- **Universal signage** — readable across cultures (Washington Metro, NYC subway)
- **Product/industrial integration** — design spans print, product, environment

## Techniques / Tools
- **Historical paste-up era** — actual physical type, grids drawn on boards
- Later: early Mac desktop publishing (QuarkXPress)
- Ink drawing / technical pens for drafting
- **Collaborated with skilled craft** — printers, typographers, photographers
- His influence persists in Adobe Illustrator's defaults (nothing fancy, flat vector, grid-snap)

## Mood / Emotional Register
Authoritative, confident, clear. Design serves reader/user; designer disappears behind system. Neither warm nor cold — **objective**, like good signage. The opposite of expressive.

## When to apply
- ✅ Wayfinding / signage systems
- ✅ Corporate identity with seriousness
- ✅ Editorial / magazine design
- ✅ Systematic design with many applications
- ✅ When project demands **objective clarity**
- ✅ Institutional / civic design
- ❌ NOT for personal expression / artistic ambiguity
- ❌ NOT for emotional / narrative work
- ❌ NOT where breaking convention is the point

## How to apply (tactical for Claude)
1. **Grid first.** Define 12-column grid with baseline. Snap everything.
2. **Typeface: Helvetica** (default) or one of the Six. NO display fonts, NO script, NO distressed.
3. **Palette: red + black + white.** Maximum 3 colors in entire piece.
4. **NO gradients. Ever.** Flat color fills only.
5. **Hierarchy through size + position + whitespace**, not through decoration.
6. **Headline tight letter-spacing, body neutral tracking** — consistent choices.
7. **Generous margins** — breathing room is structural.
8. **Every element on grid** — no freehand placement.
9. **Information layer** — readable at a glance, then details discoverable.
10. **NOT to use:** script fonts, gradients, hand-drawn elements, overlapping type, asymmetric-emotional composition, decorative borders. Vignelli's system would reject all these as failures of discipline.
